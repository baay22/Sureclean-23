How To Clean Curtains At Home Using 3 Easy Methods

Turning your house into a stylish and inviting oasis requires keen attention to even the minutest of details, inclusive of having clean curtains. Should the question on attaining clean curtains at home trouble you, fret not. It’s simple and budget-friendly. Apart from enhancing your home’s aesthetic appeal, curtains also double up as a barrier against exterior dust and dirt. However, with time, these curtains are prone to accumulate dirt, spots, and even disagreeable smells. This accumulation of filth can trigger health problems and hamper the overall mood of your room.

Fortunately, cleaning curtains at home is an easy and affordable process that requires minimal effort. With a few effective techniques, you can keep your curtains looking fresh and new for a long time. So keep reading to learn more!

Types of Curtains Based on Materials

Type of Curtain Description
Cotton Curtains Durable, versatile, and affordable; can be washed using a washing machine or by hand.
Silk Curtains Luxurious and delicate; best dry cleaned to prevent damage to the fabric.
Linen Curtains Natural and breathable; it can be machine-washed or hand washed but requires careful ironing.
Polyester Curtains Durable, easy to care for, and resistant to wrinkles and shrinking; it can be machine-washed or hand washed.
Sheer Curtains Lightweight and translucent; available in different materials such as cotton, polyester, and silk.
Lace Curtains Delicate and intricate; best hand washed or dry-cleaned to prevent damage to the fabric.

Cleaning Techniques Specific to Each Type of Curtains

Cotton Curtains

  • Wash with mild detergent and cold water, and tumble dry on low heat or hang to dry.
  • Iron on low if needed.

Silk Curtains

  • Dry clean only and use a steamer to remove wrinkles.
  • Avoid getting the fabric wet.

Linen Curtains

  • Wash with mild detergent and cold water, and hang to dry.
  • Iron while slightly damp to avoid wrinkles.

Polyester Curtains

  • Wash with mild detergent and cold water, and tumble dry on low heat or hang to dry.
  • Remove wrinkles by steaming or ironing on low.

Sheer Curtains

  • Wash with mild detergent and cold water, and hang to dry or tumble dry on low heat.
  • Avoid using bleach or fabric softener.

Lace Curtains

  • Hand wash with mild detergent and cold water, and lay flat to dry or hang on a non-metal hanger.
  • Avoid using bleach or fabric softener and iron on low if needed.

How to Prepare for Curtain Cleaning

  • Check the care label on the curtains for specific cleaning instructions and warnings.
  • Remove any rods, hooks, or other hardware attached to the curtains before cleaning.
  • Use a soft brush attachment to dust off the curtains and a vacuum cleaner to remove loose dirt or dust.
  • Test a small, inconspicuous area for color bleeding or fading during cleaning.
  • Spot treats any stains using a mild detergent and a soft brush or cloth.
  • Prepare the cleaning solution as instructed on the care label or using mild detergent and cold water.
  • Select the appropriate cleaning method based on the curtains’ type and cleaning requirements.
  • Follow the cleaning instructions carefully, and avoid using harsh chemicals or fabric softeners.
  • Dry the curtains as recommended on the care label, either by hanging or tumbling on low heat. Avoid direct sunlight or high heat.

How to Clean Curtains at Home

✓ Handwashing Curtains

To hand wash curtains, clean the bathtub or basin with cool water and mild detergent. Soak the curtains for 15-20 minutes, gently swishing them to remove dirt and stains. Drain the soapy water and refill the tub with cool water to rinse the curtains.

Swish them around to remove any remaining soap, then gently press out any excess water. Hang the curtains on a clothesline or drying rack to air dry, smoothing out any wrinkles or creases by hand as they dry.

Always check the care label instructions before washing curtains, and avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments that can damage the fabric.

✓ Machine washing curtain

To machine wash curtains, check the care label instructions first to make sure they’re machine washable. Remove any hooks or hardware and place the curtains in the machine, taking care not to overload it.

Add a mild detergent and set the machine to a gentle cycle with cold water. Once the cycle is complete, remove the curtains and gently press out any excess water. Hang them on a clothesline or drying rack to air dry, smoothing out any wrinkles or creases by hand.

Avoid using fabric softeners or bleach, and always follow the care label instructions to avoid damaging the curtains.

✓ Dry Cleaning

To dry clean curtains, check the care label instructions to make sure they’re suitable for dry cleaning. Remove any hooks or hardware and take the curtains to a reputable dry cleaner that specializes in curtain cleaning. Let the dry cleaner know the material and any specific cleaning instructions or stains.

The dry cleaner will use a gentle solvent to clean the curtains, then press and steam them to remove any wrinkles. Once the curtains are cleaned, hang them on a clothesline or drying rack to air out any remaining solvent smell and smooth out any wrinkles or creases by hand as they dry.

Note that dry cleaning curtains can be expensive, but it’s a good choice for delicate or heavily soiled curtains that can’t be washed at home. Always follow the care label instructions and choose a reputable dry cleaner for the best results.

Homemade Curtain Cleaning Solutions

✱ Vinegar and Water Solution

Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Spray the solution on the curtains and wait a few minutes. Then, wipe the curtains with a clean, damp cloth or sponge.

✱ Baking Soda Solution

Mix 2 tablespoons of baking soda with a cup of warm water. Apply the solution to stained areas and let it sit for 30 minutes. Rinse and air-dry the curtains.

✱ Lemon Juice and Salt Solution

Mix 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 cup of boiling water. Apply the solution to the curtains and wait 30 minutes. Rinse the curtains with warm water and air dry.

✱ Cornstarch Solution

Mix 2 cups of cold water with 1/4 cup of cornstarch.

Apply the solution to the curtains and let them dry completely. Once the solution is dry, use a clean brush to remove the cornstarch from the curtains.

✱ Club Soda Solution

Pour club soda on a clean cloth and gently rub it on the curtains. Let the curtains dry completely before hanging them back up.

Tips for Maintaining Clean Curtains at Home

  • Regularly dust curtains with a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment to prevent dirt buildup.
  • Quickly spot clean any stains or spills using a gentle cleaner and blotting the area.
  • Avoid hanging damp curtains to prevent mold and mildew growth.
  • Keep curtains away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and damage. Use sheer curtains or blinds to filter sunlight.
  • Follow care label instructions when washing or dry cleaning curtains, and avoid using harsh detergents or fabric softeners.
  • Regularly inspect curtains for wear and tear and replace them if necessary to maintain appearance and functionality.

Conclusion

To keep your curtains looking their best, it’s important to understand their type and material, as well as the right cleaning technique to use. Maintain your curtains by handwashing, machine washing, and dry cleaning them.

Preventive actions include dusting, spot cleaning, avoiding direct sunlight, and checking for wear and tear. By doing so, you can prolong their lifespan and ensure they remain a beautiful addition to your home decor.
